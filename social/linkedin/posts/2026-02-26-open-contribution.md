Fitness standards shouldn't be owned by one company.

That's the whole point of OpenProgression. The standard is MIT licensed, the data is public, and every benchmark traces back to peer-reviewed research. But we can't build this alone.

We need coaches who see athletes every day. Researchers who know the data. Developers who build the tools. Athletes who live the progression.

Here's how you can contribute:

Review the benchmarks. Are the numbers accurate for your experience? We have 25 movements across 8 categories. If something feels off, tell us.

Suggest movements. The library is growing. If your sport or discipline is underrepresented, help us fix that.

Improve the methodology. Know a study we missed? Have data that could make the standard more accurate? We want it.

Build integrations. The data is JSON, MIT licensed, and ready to plug into any app, platform, or coaching tool.

Or just share this with someone who cares about getting fitness standards right.

The best standard is one that the whole community builds together.
